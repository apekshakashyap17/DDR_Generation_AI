1. PROPERTY ISSUE SUMMARY

The property shows visible signs of water leakage and dampness in several internal rooms and also minor external wall damage. Moisture is actively present, causing damp patches, peeling paint, and some efflorescence in multiple bedrooms, kitchen, living areas and bathrooms. External wall cracks and plumbing issues have contributed to these problems. Overall, seven distinct impacted areas were identified, including hall, bedrooms, kitchen, master bedroom, parking area, and common bathrooms.

Most issues are moderate to high severity and are active leaks rather than just historic stains. The presence of plumbing leaks concealed within walls, damaged traps under tiles, and gaps in tile joints are allowing water to continually seep and affect surrounding walls and floors. Thermal scans confirm ongoing moisture in various room corners and wall bases, indicating rising damp and active water ingress in internal spaces.

Given the continuous leakage and moisture presence linked to plumbing and tile joint defects, timely intervention is essential to prevent further deterioration, mold formation, and possible damage to finishes and structure. Prompt repairs to plumbing joints, tile sealing and wall cracks will reduce moisture sources and restore property condition.

3. PROBABLE ROOT CAUSE

Property Overall:  
The main cause of the widespread dampness and leakage problems is defective concealed plumbing joints and damaged drainage components such as the Nahani trap under tile flooring. These failures allow continuous water leakage which infiltrates tile joints, floor skirting, walls, and ceilings. Gaps in tile joints and cracks in external walls further facilitate water ingress. The issues appear persistent and have likely existed for a considerable period, as indicated by efflorescence, peeling paint, rusting plumbing joints, and extensive damp patches.

Area-wise, each affected room’s dampness traces back to the adjacent bathroom plumbing and floor tile deficiencies, showing a systemic maintenance failure in plumbing and sealing workmanship rather than age-related wear alone. External wall cracks contribute to moisture ingress in the master bedroom external wall, likely due to unaddressed structural cracking. The problem has spread from bathrooms and concealed plumbing routes vertically and horizontally into living spaces, indicating poor waterproofing and maintenance.

5. RECOMMENDED ACTIONS

Priority 1 — Repair Concealed Plumbing Leaks  
Area affected: Hall, Bedroom, Master Bedroom, Kitchen, Common Bathroom  
What to do: Engage a qualified plumber to locate and repair all leaking concealed plumbing joints and damaged Nahani trap under tile flooring. Replace rusted joints and restore watertight seals.  
Why urgent: Active plumbing leaks are the root cause of moisture damage and will worsen if unrepaired, causing structural damage and health risks.  
Estimated complexity: Complex  

Priority 2 — Re-seal and Repair Tile Joints  
Area affected: Hall, Bedroom, Master Bedroom, Kitchen, Common Bathroom  
What to do: Remove and replace hollow or damaged tiles; grout and seal all tile joints properly to prevent water ingress. Ensure tiling fixes prevent water seepage under flooring.  
Why urgent: Gaps and hollowness allow water leakage to persist even after plumbing repairs, perpetuating damage.  
Estimated complexity: Moderate  

Priority 3 — Repair External Wall Cracks  
Area affected: Master Bedroom External Wall  
What to do: Inspect and seal external wall cracks; apply suitable water-resistant coatings or plaster to prevent water ingress.  
Why urgent: External cracks worsen internal dampness and compromise wall integrity if unaddressed.  
Estimated complexity: Moderate  

Priority 4 — Address Ceiling Seepage in Parking Area  
Area affected: Parking Area Ceiling  
What to do: Locate water ingress source and repair plumbing or drainage causing seepage; treat ceiling surface for damp damage and repaint if necessary.  
Why urgent: Unrepaired seepage can cause ceiling deterioration and potential fall hazards.  
Estimated complexity: Moderate  

Priority 5 — Clean and Treat Mold and Damp Stained Surfaces  
Area affected: All interiors with visible dampness  
What to do: After fixing leaks, treat affected walls with anti-fungal agents and repaint using suitable moisture-resistant paints. Regularly clean areas prone to moisture.  
Why urgent: Prevents mold growth and health hazards.  
Estimated complexity: Simple
